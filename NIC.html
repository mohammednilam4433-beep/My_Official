National Identity Card (NIC) Application

Apply for your National Identity Card - Your official proof of identity in Sri Lanka

Required Documents

Documents Needed for NIC Application

1. Application Form

Duly completed and certified by the Grama Niladhari (GN) of your division and countersigned by the Divisional Secretary (DS) or another authorized officer.

2. Birth Certificate (Mandatory)

Original or a certified copy issued by the Registrar General's Department.

For those born abroad, the Consular Birth Certificate or other approved birth document.

3. Citizenship Certificate (if applicable)

Required if your citizenship is not clearly established by the birth certificate (e.g., if parents are foreign nationals).

4. Supporting Documents to Prove Identity and Residence

One or more of the following (depending on availability):

  • Educational Certificates (GCE O/L, A/L, Degree, etc.)
  • Driving License
  • Passport
  • Electoral Register extracts
  • Marriage Certificate (if name change after marriage)
  • Service/Employment Identity Card (for government/private sector employees)
  • Affidavit or letter certified by GN/JP when no other documents are available.

5. Photographs

Two passport-size color photos (recent, within 6 months) that meet DRP specifications.

Must be certified by GN or authorized officer.

6. Special Situations

  • Lost NIC: A police report and/or affidavit may be required.
  • Damaged NIC: Old/damaged NIC must be submitted.
  • Name Change: Gazette notification or marriage certificate required.
  • First-time NIC at 15+ years: Birth certificate and school certificate are usually mandatory.

Important:

  • Every person who is a citizen of Sri Lanka and who has attained or attains the age of 15 years shall apply for a National Identity card.
  • All documents must be originals or certified true copies.
  • Applications are usually submitted via the Grama Niladhari or Divisional Secretariat, not directly to the Department.
